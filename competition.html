National Undergraduate Mathematical Modeling Competition

Result:

Second Prize in Shanghai Division

Objective:

The goal of this competition was to devise a replenishment and pricing strategy for six vegetable categories based on sales and pricing data, loss rates, and historical trends from July 1, 2020, to June 30, 2023. This involved analyzing sales details, wholesale prices, and loss data to make data-driven decisions about inventory management and pricing strategies for a supermarket chain.

Solution: Seasonal Regression Model & Linear Programming Optimization
  • Developed a seasonal regression model to predict sales trends for each vegetable category, taking into account seasonal variations and historical data.
  • Utilized linear programming optimization to determine the most cost-effective replenishment quantities and pricing strategies, minimizing waste and maximizing revenue.
  • Addressed missing data using interpolation techniques and time-series forecasting to ensure robust predictions despite gaps in historical sales data.
  • Incorporated historical loss rate analysis to adjust replenishment strategies based on product shelf life and loss rates, ensuring that inventory is optimized for both sales and waste reduction.

UAV Freshman Cup - Technology Innovation Drone Competition

Result:

Second Prize

Objective:

The goal of this competition was to develop a drone flight control system incorporating gesture recognition, Aruco code detection, and circle detection, allowing the drone to autonomously fly through predefined circles and land precisely on a target without collision.

Solution: Drone Flight Control with Gesture, Aruco Code, and Circle Detection
  • Implemented Hough Circle detection in a simulation environment to identify circular markers and guide the drone through specific waypoints.
  • Successfully integrated gesture recognition and Aruco marker detection on the Robomaster TT drone, enabling intuitive control and navigation using hand gestures and marker-based positioning.
  • Achieved autonomous drone flight, navigating through all circles without collision and landing precisely on the target using real-time visual feedback from the drone’s cameras.
  • Tested and refined the system in real-world environments, ensuring consistent performance and robustness in various conditions.

Freshman Cup Structural Design Competition

Result:

Third Prize

Objective:

The goal of this competition was to design a structural model capable of withstanding vertical static and impact loads. The task was to catch a sandbag falling from a certain height and transport it to a specified area. The model's score was determined by the ratio of the total number of sandbags caught to the model's weight, as well as the competition time.

Solution: Box-Type Structure with Four Supports and a Fixed Plate
  • Designed a box-type structure to effectively distribute vertical static and dynamic loads, ensuring stability under both static and impact conditions.
  • Incorporated four support pillars strategically positioned to enhance load-bearing capacity and provide structural integrity during the impact load phase.
  • Developed a fixed plate system to securely hold the sandbag, while minimizing the deformation caused by both vertical static loads and the impact force from the falling sandbag.
  • Conducted detailed stress analysis using mechanical principles, ensuring the structure’s ability to withstand repeated impacts while maintaining the integrity of the entire model.
